Transportation

Link Light Rail

Seattle’s Link Light Rail offers a fast and convenient way to travel between major destinations like the airport, downtown, and the University of Washington. The system is expanding rapidly, providing a clean and efficient alternative to driving.

King County Metro Buses

Metro buses are Seattle’s backbone of public transit, covering hundreds of routes that reach neighborhoods across King County. Equipped with accessibility features, real-time tracking, and bike racks, they make commuting simple and sustainable.

Washington State Ferries

Ferries are an iconic part of Seattle’s transportation scene, connecting the city to nearby islands and the Olympic Peninsula. With beautiful views of Puget Sound, they are both a practical and scenic way to travel.

Bike Share and Cycling

Seattle’s bike lanes and trails make it easy to get around on two wheels. Bike-sharing programs like Lime and Veo allow riders to rent bikes on demand, promoting a healthy and eco-friendly way to explore the city.

Seattle Water Taxi

The Seattle Water Taxi connects downtown with West Seattle and Vashon Island, offering a peaceful commute across Elliott Bay. It's a unique mode of transport for both daily commuters and tourists enjoying the waterfront skyline.

Seattle Streetcar

The modern Seattle Streetcar connects key urban districts like South Lake Union and Capitol Hill. It’s a great way to move through the heart of the city while reducing congestion and emissions.
