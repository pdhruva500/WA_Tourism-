Famous Landmarks

Mount Rainier

Location: Mount Rainier National Park, Washington

Mount Rainier National Park is a stunning destination for travelers seeking natural beauty and adventure. Towering at 14,410 feet, Mount Rainier is an active volcano surrounded by alpine meadows, waterfalls, and glaciers, making it a perfect place for hiking, sightseeing, and photography.

Space Needle

Location: Seattle, Washington

One of the most photographed and recognized structures in the world, the Space Needle was built as a cultural centerpiece for the 1962 World’s Fair. At 605 feet tall, the landmark symbolizes Seattle’s innovative and forward-thinking spirit.

Puget Sound

Location: Puget Sound, Washington

Puget Sound is a stunning network of waterways, inlets, and islands that offers a unique blend of natural beauty and urban charm. Visitors can enjoy ferry rides, whale watching, and scenic hikes with breathtaking views of the Pacific Northwest.
